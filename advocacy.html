ADVOCACY AND POPULAR EDUCATION FOR GOOD GOVERNANCE (PEGG)

ISACC’s advocacy work has deep historical roots, culminating in the mobilization of church communities during the resistance to the Marcos regime. We organized Konfes (Konsensiya ng Febrero Siete), a coalition that became a center of evangelical presence at the barricades during the February 1986 People Power uprising.

Today, our long-term Popular Education for Good Governance (PEGG) program continues this legacy. A unique feature of our work is the integration of Biblical perspectives into the analysis of complex issues, intentionally training young people to become biblically literate and apply God’s Word to the public square.

Faith and Politics Initiative

During the 2022 Presidential Election, we launched a major webinar series on “Faith and Politics.” In partnership with Evangelical scholars and experts, we aimed to help Christians engage in national transformation based on sound biblical-theological teachings.

This initiative is a collaboration with the PCEC Justice, Peace and Reconciliation Commission (JPARCOM) and Langham Publishing (UK). The series will result in a book that addresses the need for contextualized resources in a region where most seminaries rely on Western textbooks, hoping to be a valuable contribution to the conversation on theology and politics in the Philippines and Asia.

Media Production for Social Change

Our multi-media shows serve as culture-friendly learning tools for a people whose cognitive style is largely visual, acting as artistic statements of our commitment to wholistic transformation. Our productions include:

  • The Filipino’s Revolution: A 15-minute analysis of the February 1986 Revolution in light of the Filipino’s socio-psychological makeup.
  • Pag-aalay: A 27-minute show exploring Filipino religious beliefs and their connection to the Gospel.
  • Kapag Nahawi ang Ulap sa Mukha ng Diyos: A complex, multi-projector show retelling gospel stories in a contemporary Filipino context.
  • Onse de Mayo (May Eleven): A 15-minute documentary discussing personality-based vs. issue-based electoral systems and how to choose leaders wisely.
  • Statue Square: A 17-minute show on the situation of Filipina domestic helpers in Hongkong, questioning the viability of the migrant labor industry.
  • Dangal ng Bansa (Pride of the Nation): A 20-minute show tackling nationalism from biblical perspectives to stimulate the Church’s participation in national affairs.
  • Media Today: Tomorrow’s Society?: A 7-minute show presenting the state of Philippine media and its effect in shaping society.

A Legacy of Engagement: Key Fora & Seminars

Throughout our history, ISACC has stimulated critical thinking by engaging churches, grassroots communities, and the public on vital issues. Notable events include:

  • Philippine Realities Conferences (1981, 1985): Stimulated church responses to authoritarian rule.
  • Seminar on Philippine Socio-Cultural History (1984): Featured nationalist thinker Renato Constantino.
  • Courses on Social Change & Christian Ethics (1985): Led by Dr. John Howard Yoder, a leading thinker on active non-violence.
  • Theology Encounters Revolution (1987): A course on theology in a revolutionary situation by Andrew Kirk of the London Institute for Contemporary Christianity.
  • Lecture on The State as Servant and Subversive: Featured Dr. Ronald Sider, author of Rich Christians in an Age of Hunger.
  • Public Symposia: Numerous lectures on human rights, vigilantism, national debt, insurgency, and other pressing issues, led by national experts.
