Research

Informing Transformation Through Principled Research.

Knowledge is the foundation of effective action. ISACC is frequently tapped for its expertise in development and socio-cultural issues. Our research provides faith-based organizations and leaders with the critical insights needed to address complex challenges from a biblical perspective, evaluating both technical efficacy and missional impact.

Featured Publications from Research

A Clash of Cultures

A groundbreaking, PEW-funded research into the intercultural miscues between American Protestant Missions and Filipino religious consciousness at the turn of the century. Published by De La Salle University and Anvil Press.

Rise Up and Walk

An ICCO-funded critique of development narratives, offering practical insights into wholistic development based on participatory research in seven poor communities. Published by Regnum and the Oxford Center for Mission Studies.

Notable Research & Documentation

Conversion to Protestant Christianity Under Early American Rule (1995-1999)

A massive four-year study of the interface between Filipino religious consciousness and the conversion themes brought by American Protestant missionaries.

Core Insights:
  • Examined cross-cultural problems arising from missions arriving with an imperial power.
  • Analyzed the culture-fit between missionary messages and the indigenous religious imagination using oral histories, archival sources, and textual analysis.
  • The first study of its kind funded by the PEW Foundation outside US territory and conducted entirely by local scholars.

Project Evaluation & Consultancy for NGOs

ISACC provides project evaluation and consultancy services to assess the impact and efficacy of mission and development organizations. Notable studies include:

Project Highlights:
  • Culturally-Sensitive Exit Strategies: A study for Tear Fund UK involving case studies in Vietnam, Cambodia, and Thailand to analyze the impact of project exits on local partners.
  • Impact Assessment in Tacloban: An evaluation of disaster interventions by three faith-based organizations, commissioned by Woord en Daad, Netherlands.
  • Transforming Communities (CCT): Research into how the Center for Community Transformation, a microfinance NGO, integrates faith and practice in its operations.
  • Faith-Based Organizations & Poverty Reduction: A baseline study focused on the community development contributions of evangelical organizations.

Homeroom: An Evaluation (1982-1985)

A three-year nationwide research for the Ministry of Education (funded through the World Bank) on the effectiveness of homeroom activities for value education in elementary schools.

Core Insights:
  • Provided baseline data for the national Program for Decentralized Education (PRODED).
  • Formed the basis for institutionalizing an integrated values education program in the national curriculum.

Documenting Development Learnings

ISACC is committed to sharing insights from its fieldwork through publications and resource manuals. Key projects include:

Highlighted Projects:
  • Hasik-Unlad (1998): Documented insights from training church-based communities, integrating biblical perspectives with the community organizing framework.
  • Sambahaginan (1991): Documented the community organizing process and development learnings from Sitio Ibayo, Nasugbu, Batangas.
  • Kingdom Agenda (1980): A World Vision-funded development resource manual to assist Christian organizations in moving towards more contextual and wholistic work.

The Trade Union Movement in the Philippines (1979)

A history of the labor movement commissioned by the National Federation of Labor Unions (NAFLU). The research was primarily based on extensive oral history interviews with the late labor leader Felixberto Olalia, founder of the Kilusang Mayo Uno.
