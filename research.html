Historical Research & Documentation

Conversion to Protestant Christianity Under Early American Rule (1995-1999)

A massive four-year study of the interface between Filipino religious consciousness and conversion themes brought by American missionaries.

Core Insights:
  • Examined cross-cultural problems arising from missions arriving with an imperial power.
  • Analyzed the culture-fit between missionary messages and indigenous religious imagination.
  • The first study of its kind funded by the PEW Foundation outside US territory.

Homeroom: An Evaluation (1982-1985)

A three-year nationwide research for the Ministry of Education on the effectiveness of homeroom activities for value education in elementary schools.

Core Insights:
  • Provided baseline data for the national Program for Decentralized Education (PRODED).
  • Formed the basis for institutionalizing an integrated values education program.

Project Evaluation Studies for International NGOs

ISACC provides project evaluation and consultancy services. Notable studies include a study on culturally-sensitive exit strategies for Tear Fund UK and an impact assessment of disaster interventions for Woord en Daad, Netherlands.
